Harvard Byzantine Greek Reading List — Sourced Edition

Every item on Harvard Classics' PhD reading list for Byzantine Greek, with the original Greek text and a free/non-commercial source located wherever one exists, commercial publisher links otherwise. Compiled June 2026.

Verified free, non-commercial source Free general collection (Patrologia Graeca, Bonn Corpus, or similar 19th-c. edition) Commercial / publisher source Best lead found — not independently confirmed GR contains the original Greek
How to read this list. Every entry below now includes a source that gives you the Greek text itself, not just a translation — marked with the green GR tag. Where I could find a free Greek source, it's listed first; gloss/translation language preference (English, then French) is applied after that.

Update: a second pass through this list, searching titles in Greek script rather than English/transliteration, recovered several sources that the first pass missed entirely — most dramatically the Lament for Constantinople, which yielded nothing under its English title but turned up a full critical text within seconds once searched as Ανακάλημα της Κωνσταντινόπολης. Items recovered this way are noted explicitly. A genuine handful of items still have no confirmed free Greek source despite searching properly in Greek — these are specialist texts whose only edition is a 20th/21st-century critical apparatus (Teubner, Sources Chrétiennes, Brepols) that simply isn't digitized anywhere open; for those I give the standard scholarly edition rather than inventing a link.

Alexios Makrembolites — Dialogue between Rich and Poor
Re-searched directly in Greek (Διάλογος πλουσίων και πενήτων) — still no free full text located. Full title: Διάλογος πλουσίων καὶ πενήτων· τίνας ἂν εἶπον λόγους πένητες πρὸς πλουσίους καὶ τίνας ἂν πρὸς τοὺς πένητας οὗτοι, written Constantinople, Oct.–Nov. 1343. The Greek text was first edited by Ihor Ševčenko in "Alexios Makrembolites and his 'Dialogue between the Rich and the Poor'," Zbornik Radova Vizantološkog Instituta 6 (1960), 187–228 — check JSTOR or your university's database for that journal issue.

Δημώδης Γραμματεία / Centre for the Greek Language vernacular literature portal GR
Free dimodis.greeklanguage.gr
A state-run (Greek Ministry of Education / Centre for the Greek Language) digital portal for medieval and early modern vernacular Greek literature — texts, scholarly introductions, and often full PDFs. This single resource resolved several entries in this list that turned up nothing when searched in English: the Lament for Constantinople, Ptochoprodromos, Kallimachos and Chrysorrhoe, and the Entertaining Tale of Quadrupeds. Worth browsing directly for any other vernacular Byzantine text — its catalog also covers Digenes Akrites, Belthandros and Chrysantza, Livistros and Rodamne, the Chronicle of the Morea, and more.
Patrologia Graeca (Migne, 161 vols., Greek + Latin) GR
Free Documenta Catholica Omnia — site map, browsable by author
Free Complete set also mirrored on archive.org (search "Patrologia Graeca volume [number]")
Covers: Basil, Gregory of Nazianzus, Andrew of Crete, Eusebius, Synesius, John Moschos, Leontios of Neapolis, Photios, George Pisides, Symeon Metaphrastes, Niketas Choniates, Theophanes, John Malalas, Anna Komnene, and most other patristic/historical authors on this list, in the original Greek with facing Latin translation.
Corpus Scriptorum Historiae Byzantinae ("Bonn Corpus," 50 vols., Greek + Latin) GR
Free Archive.org — individual volumes (search the series title; there is no single full-set scan)
Covers the standard 19th-century Greek editions of: Niketas Choniates, Anna Komnene, Theophanes, John Malalas, George Pisides, John Kinnamos, George Kedrenos, Constantine VII, and most other Byzantine chronicle/historical writers on this list.
Hercher's Erotici Scriptores Graeci (1858–59, Greek only) GR
Free Archive.org — vol. 1 (Tomus prior, 1858): Parthenius, Achilles Tatius, Longus, Xenophon of Ephesus, etc.
The Byzantine prose romances are in vol. 2 (Tomus alter, 1859): Eumathios (Eustathios) Makrembolites' Hysmine and Hysminias, Theodore Prodromos' Rhodanthe and Dosikles, and Niketas Eugenianos — but only vol. 1 (the ancient novelists) is digitized on archive.org, so vol. 2 must be consulted via HathiTrust or a library.
Wagner's Carmina Graeca Medii Aevi (1874, Greek text of vernacular poems) GR
Free Archive.org
Covers a range of vernacular medieval Greek poems including the Ptochoprodromika; check its table of contents directly for exact coverage against your specific reading-list entries.
Pitra's Analecta Sacra Spicilegio Solesmensi parata (1876, vol. 1, Greek hymns) GR
Free Archive.org
Contains the Greek text of 29 kontakia of Romanos Melodos, using a different (older) numbering than the standard modern Maas–Trypanis edition.
